> About me

The keyboard is my paintbrush, my text editor is my canvas, and my language & framework are my medium. In my opinion, programming is a functional form of art, which has the ability to change the lives of people around the world.

Ever since writing my first C++ program in my into to programming class, I became obsessed with endless possibilities and solutions software had to offer. Software engineering is a never ending puzzle that I am passionately engaged in solving.

For my first professional experience as a programmer, I was given the opportunity to lead the front-end development of a small startup’s web application. This opportunity was monumental for me, as it radically changed my initial perceptions of professional programming. It was not some dull grey office job, it was an exciting and engaging experience that constantly challenged me to learn, creative, problem solve, design, and ultimately grow as a programmer and person.

After a year of work experience, I returned to school where I needed to declare my engineering major. My overwhelmingly positive CO-OP experience as a developer made it and easy decision, and I chose Computer Engineering.

Currently, I’m in my final year of Computer Engineering at Simon Fraser University where I am also a student-athlete on our NCAA DII SFU Men’s American Football team as a Redshirt Senior Quarterback.
